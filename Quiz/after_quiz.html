Before we start, let me remind you that:

  • The length of a match is randomly determined. After every round there is a probability that the match will continue for another round. The probability depends on your choice and a choice of the person you are paired with. You will interact with the same person for the entire match.
  • This is how the length of a match is determined: at the end of each round, the computer rolls a fair 6-sided die.
    • When both you and the person you are matched with chose 1, then if the die comes up 5 or more we do not continue to another round, and the match is over. If the die comes up 4 or less, we continue to another round of the same match.
    • When action 2 was chosen at least once, then if the die comes up 4 or more we do not continue to another round, and the match is over. If the die comes up 3 or less, we continue to another round of the same match.
  • Your payoff in each round depend on these choices.
  • Your choice and the choice of the person you are paired with will be shown to both of you at the end of the round
  • After a match is finished, you will be randomly paired with someone for a new match.
